Build Impact OS as a true investor-grade and grantmaker-grade impact reporting platform, not a generic SDG report generator.

The current report structure feels too academic, overly focused on SDG descriptions, and contains large blocks of AI-generated text. Instead, redesign the platform to resemble modern impact reports published by leading social enterprises, foundations, accelerators, and B-Corps.

The experience should begin with an onboarding flow where users enter their organization information:

* Organization Name
* Logo
* Organization Type
* Industry
* Country / Region
* Reporting Period
* Website
* Mission Statement
* Beneficiary Groups
* Key Programs
* Key Metrics

The organization name should be used throughout the report so every report feels personalized and unique.

The generated report should focus on evidence, outcomes, impact metrics, growth, and accountability rather than lengthy explanations.

Replace long essay-style sections with visual storytelling, dashboards, KPI cards, charts, timelines, maps, beneficiary stories, and outcome-focused summaries.

The report should follow this structure:

1. Cover Page

* Organization Logo
* Organization Name
* Impact Report Year
* Mission Statement
* Hero Metric Banner
* Professional annual report aesthetic

2. Impact At A Glance

* Beneficiaries Reached
* Programs Delivered
* Partnerships Formed
* Volunteers Engaged
* Funds Raised
* Communities Served
* Geographic Reach
* Year-over-Year Growth
* Visual KPI cards
* Timeline of major achievements

3. Organization Overview

* Mission
* Vision
* Problem Being Addressed
* Target Beneficiaries
* Brief narrative only
* Maximum 250 words

4. Programs & Initiatives
   Generate a dedicated section for every program.

Each program should include:

* Program Name
* Objective
* Activities
* Outputs
* Outcomes
* Beneficiaries
* Metrics
* Optional images

5. Beneficiary Impact
   This should become one of the largest sections.

Include:

* Beneficiary Profiles
* Success Stories
* Testimonials
* Before vs After Comparisons
* Human-centered storytelling
* Outcome evidence

The platform should generate realistic impact stories based on user inputs.

6. Impact Dashboard
   Generate visual analytics including:

* Growth charts
* Progress bars
* KPI cards
* Geographic maps
* Trend analysis
* Impact distribution charts

Metrics should adapt to organization type.

Examples:

Education:

* Students Reached
* Schools Served
* Mentorship Hours
* Scholarships Awarded

Startup:

* Customers Served
* Revenue Impact
* Jobs Created
* Product Adoption

Climate:

* CO₂ Reduced
* Energy Saved
* Waste Diverted

Healthcare:

* Patients Reached
* Treatments Delivered
* Health Outcomes

7. Theory of Change
   Redesign completely.

Do not generate long paragraphs.

Create a visual framework:

Inputs → Activities → Outputs → Outcomes → Long-Term Impact

Each stage should contain concise bullet points and visual flow diagrams.

8. Measurement Framework
   Show:

* Metrics Tracked
* Baselines
* Targets
* Current Progress
* Data Sources
* Collection Frequency

Display as tables and dashboards rather than paragraphs.

9. Challenges & Learnings
   Include:

* Key Challenges
* Risks
* Lessons Learned
* Adaptations Made
* Future Improvements

Use concise cards and callout sections.

10. Future Commitments
    Include:

* Next Year Goals
* Strategic Roadmap
* Expansion Plans
* Long-Term Vision

Display as milestones and timelines.

11. Appendix

* Organization Details
* Contact Information
* Methodology
* Data Sources
* Reporting Notes

Generation Rules:

* Maximum 30% text
* Minimum 70% visual content
* Use cards instead of paragraphs whenever possible
* Use charts wherever metrics exist
* Use timelines instead of long narrative sections
* Use comparison tables instead of repetitive text
* Every claim should be connected to a metric
* Avoid generic sustainability jargon
* Avoid excessive SDG explanations
* Avoid academic essay writing
* Avoid filler text

Most importantly, the report must dynamically adapt to the organization.

An AI startup, nonprofit, student initiative, climate organization, health project, education program, or social enterprise should all generate different report structures, metrics, visuals, and narratives.

The final result should feel closer to a modern annual report from Acumen, Ashoka, Skoll Foundation, Gates Foundation, YC-backed social ventures, and leading B-Corp companies rather than a generic SDG assignment.
